InkLink: A Mobile App Casestudy

Provide a mobile app for individuals who are new to tattoos to connect with tattoo artists by providing matches with local artists. The app will simplify the process of finding the right tattoo artist, increasing the likelihood of following through with getting a tattoo, and decreasing the likelihood of regret over it.

Overview

Role

UX UI Researcher and Designer on a 3-person team project.

Tools

Figma, Figma Jam, Trello, Google Drive

Deliverable

Research, prototype, test, and validate a new mobile app that solves a real, clearly identified need in a user's everyday lives.

Case Study

User Research

Created a Proto Persona, drafted a research plan and conducted research, including five interviews and one survey. Then, analyzed user research, completed an affinity diagram, user empathy map and journey map. Then, created a user hypothesis statement and user persona.

Problem Statement

InkLink was designed to simplify the daunting journey to find the right artist for your tattoo. We have observed that there are very few resources to support the process of finding an artist, especially for people who are newer to tattoos. Potential tattooees may end up too overwhelmed by the process to carry out the tattoo, or arenâ€™t able to build a strong connection with the artists. How might we provide a space for our users to view artwork, connect with the artists and have a more approachable and fun experience when getting tattooed?

Iterations

Matching, Favoriting, and Connecting are the three key features. Usability testing reavled three areas of focus for improvement. Language: clarify the tattoo connection process; Notifications: show the value of opting into the notification amd chat feature; oversimplification: ensure we are addressing all of the user's needs.
